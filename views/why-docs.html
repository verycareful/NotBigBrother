Did we get it wrong?

The age estimation model used in this demo isn't perfect. If you think it got your age wrong, here's the honest story of why we can't just ask for your ID.

👋

We're literally just two dudes

NotBigBrother is a side project built by two people who care about online privacy. We have no legal team, no compliance infrastructure, no government agreements, and no funding to set any of that up right now.

Verifying identity documents — passports, driver's licences, national IDs — is a highly regulated space. We're not in a position to handle that responsibly yet, and we'd rather be honest about it than pretend otherwise.

Why we can't look at your documents

Handling government-issued ID requires legal authorisation in most jurisdictions — even just accepting it in transit. Getting this wrong doesn't just expose you, it exposes us to serious liability we have no infrastructure to handle.

Everything we do is designed to be transient — no storage, no logs, nothing persists. But even with the best intentions, accepting document uploads introduces a real attack surface: a compromised server could run modified source code that exfiltrates your document before it's discarded, or an attacker with server access could read it directly from memory. Until we have the infrastructure, partnerships, and resources to host and audit our own servers properly, we're not willing to take that risk with something as sensitive as a government document.

What we're working toward

As the project grows we want to explore integrations with properly audited, privacy-respecting identity verification providers — or potentially work with open standards like verifiable credentials so you can prove your age from a trusted source without us ever seeing the document itself.

If you have connections in that space or want to help, we'd genuinely love to hear from you on GitHub.

In the meantime

The model used in this demo is reasonably accurate for most adults, but it can misjudge based on lighting, photo angle, or just having one of those faces. If it got you wrong, try a well-lit front-facing photo — that tends to give the best result.

We know that's not a perfect solution. We're working on it.
